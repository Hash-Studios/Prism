# Impeccable — Prism design context

This file captures product and visual intent for **Prism** (Flutter wallpaper & home-screen setups app for Android and iOS, Hash Studios). Update the **Design Context** section when brand direction changes.

## Design Context

### Users

Prism serves **mobile users** who browse, favourite, download, and **apply** wallpapers and **setups** to their devices. A large segment **discovers** content via collections, search (keywords, colour, tags), and personalized interest feeds. **Creators** upload walls and setups, build profiles, follow others, and may pursue verification. The product also supports **Prism Premium**, **Prism Coins** (earn/spend), **AI wallpaper generation**, filters, and cloud sync via Google sign-in. Context is typically **short sessions** focused on visual browsing, quick decisions, and occasional deeper flows (upload, paywall, profile).

### Brand Personality

**Modern, premium, clean** — confident typography, generous whitespace, and restrained chrome so the product feels intentional, not busy.

**Reference:** Think **[Medium](https://medium.com)-like editorial clarity** applied to **wallpapers and design**: strong visual hierarchy, readable surfaces, calm navigation, and a sense that every screen is **curated** rather than crowded.

**Anti-patterns:** **AI slop** (generic gradients, interchangeable layouts, stock “vibes”), **forgettable** UI that could belong to any app, and **obtrusive** chrome (loud promos, clutter, competing focal points) that pulls attention off the art.

Still **open** and **community-oriented** in the product sense (uploads, follows, verification), but the **shell** should read **premium** first.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Emotional target:** **Premium polish** plus **excited discovery** — interactions feel slick and rewarding; feeds and grids should invite scrolling and saving without feeling cheap or noisy.
- **Themes:** Users choose **light** (Frost White, Coffee, Rose, Cotton Blue) or **dark** (Material Dark, AMOLED, Olive, Deep Ocean, Jungle, Pepper, Sky, Steel), plus **system** mode. Default dark mode in mapper fallback is **Material Dark**; **AMOLED** is a first-class dark variant (`prismAmoledDarkThemeId`).
- **Colour system:** Base neutrals live in `lib/theme/config.dart` (`Colors`: white/gray/black accents). **User-configurable accent** (`systemOverlayColor` in settings) overrides `ColorScheme.primary` / `error` per theme via `PrismThemeMapper`.
- **Typography:** **Proxima Nova** is the primary UI face; **Roboto** appears for some heavier title styles; **Fraunces** and **Satoshi** are bundled for selective use. See `pubspec.yaml` font entries.
- **Chrome:** `ThemeData` uses **transparent `canvasColor`** in themes — UI is built to let **imagery** dominate; avoid heavy opaque panels where a lighter treatment preserves the “gallery” feel.
- **Motion:** README promises **smooth animations**; the stack includes `animations`, `rive`, grids, and bottom navigation — motion should feel **polished** and support discovery, not gimmicky.

### Design Principles

1. **Content is the hero** — Chrome, spacing, and type should support scanning grids and full-bleed previews; do not compete with wallpapers and setups for attention.
2. **Theme fidelity** — New UI must respect the active **light/dark theme variant** and **user accent**; use `Theme.of` / `ColorScheme` and existing theme extensions rather than hard-coded colours.
3. **Performance-aware delight** — Animations and effects should reinforce clarity; align with aggressive **caching** and low-bandwidth goals from product messaging.
4. **Trust and community** — Flows for sign-in, uploads, coins, and premium should feel **fair and clear**; creator identity (profile, verification, badges) should stay scannable.
5. **Accessibility** — No formal WCAG or reduced-motion program required **for now**; revisit if priorities change. Until then, follow normal Flutter/Material touch targets and legibility without blocking shipping on audit-grade work.
