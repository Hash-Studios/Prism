# Prism Competitors Analysis

## Tier 1 — Market Leaders

### Zedge
- **Scale**: 689M+ downloads, 23M active users
- **What they do**: All-in-one personalization hub — wallpapers, ringtones, video wallpapers, notification sounds, AI generation
- **Monetization**: Free tier with ads, premium subscription, Zedge credits system, creator marketplace (Zedge Artists program)
- **Key strength**: Sheer scale and breadth of content. Hard to compete head-on.
- **AI**: Built-in text-to-image and image reimagining

### Walli
- **What they do**: Artist-first marketplace where independent artists submit wallpapers and earn money from downloads
- **Monetization**: Creator payouts (revenue sharing), premium content
- **Key strength**: The "Etsy for wallpapers" — attracts high-quality creators because they get paid
- **Takeaway for Prism**: Proves the creator economy model works for wallpapers

### Backdrops
- **What they do**: Hand-crafted wallpapers by the Backdrops team + community uploads
- **Monetization**: Premium Packs (unlockable bonus collections)
- **Key strength**: Curated quality, "Wall of the Day" feature, Material Design UI, strong Android community
- **Takeaway for Prism**: Closest competitor in terms of community + curation + Android focus

## Tier 2 — Strong Niche Players

### Abstruct
- **What they do**: Premium abstract/artistic wallpapers from Hampus Olsson (OnePlus official wallpaper designer)
- **Content**: 350+ wallpapers in 4K, themed packs (Blend, Vibrance, Craft, OnePlus, Paranoid Android, etc.)
- **Key strength**: Single designer brand identity, premium feel
- **Takeaway for Prism**: Proves the "premium artist" model works

### Resplash
- **What they do**: Open-source Unsplash client with 1M+ high-res photos
- **Features**: Auto wallpaper changer, Muzei integration, EXIF metadata, dark theme
- **Monetization**: Free and open-source (MIT License)
- **Key strength**: Auto-rotation feature, FOSS/developer community
- **Takeaway for Prism**: Auto wallpaper rotation is a low-effort, high-retention feature

### WallpapersCraft
- **What they do**: Massive catalog organized by resolution compatibility
- **Features**: 30+ categories, resolution filtering from 320x240 to 3840x2400
- **Key strength**: Resolution-first approach — every wallpaper fits every device perfectly
- **Takeaway for Prism**: Resolution/aspect-ratio filtering is expected by power users

## Tier 3 — Adjacent Competitors

### Kappboom
- 200K+ wallpaper collections, expanding into stickers and collages
- Shows the "beyond wallpapers" expansion path

### Muzei
- Not a content library but a live wallpaper *platform* with plugin architecture
- "A living museum for your home screen" — rotates famous artwork
- Open API for developers to create wallpaper sources

### AI Wallpaper Generator Apps
- Countless standalone apps using Stable Diffusion / DALL-E flooding the stores
- The biggest category threat — eating into the "browse and download" model
- Most are freemium: a few free generations, then credit-based or subscription

---

## Where Prism Fits

**Closest competitors**: Backdrops (community + curation, Android-focused) and Walli (creator community + social).

**Prism's moat**: The **Setups feature is genuinely unique** — none of these apps let you browse and share full home screen configurations (wallpaper + icon pack + widget layout). That's the differentiator to build around.

**Biggest threat**: Not any single app — it's the wave of AI wallpaper generators eating into the "browse and download" model. That's why AI generation is the highest-priority new feature.
